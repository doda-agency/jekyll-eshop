Christmas in Italy

Christmas (Natale) is one of the most important and cherished holidays in Italy. The Christmas season in Italy officially begins on December 8th with the Feast of the Immaculate Conception and continues until January 6th with the Epiphany.

Italian Christmas Traditions

Italian Christmas traditions vary by region, but some common elements include:

  • Presepi (Nativity Scenes): Creating elaborate nativity scenes is a beloved tradition throughout Italy, with Naples being particularly famous for its handcrafted figurines.
  • La Vigilia (Christmas Eve): Many Italian families celebrate with a feast of seven fishes on Christmas Eve, followed by attending Midnight Mass.
  • Babbo Natale: The Italian version of Santa Claus brings gifts to children on Christmas Eve.
  • Traditional Foods: Panettone, pandoro, and torrone are popular Christmas sweets enjoyed throughout Italy.

Regional Celebrations

Different regions of Italy have their own unique Christmas traditions:

  • Rome: The Pope delivers his "Urbi et Orbi" blessing on Christmas Day at St. Peter's Square.
  • Naples: Famous for its street of nativity scene artisans, Via San Gregorio Armeno.
  • Sicily: Celebrates with special novenas and unique pastries like buccellati.
  • Tuscany: Known for its Christmas markets and the tradition of burning a Yule log.
